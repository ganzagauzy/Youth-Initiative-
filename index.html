Our Story

In 2015 we start a group of young people who have different activities linked to broader economic development. Like the other start-up initiatives we were started as a small saving groups with a purpose of working together and becoming a group of young people using the available opportunity around us with an initial saving of 100 Rwf per week. We were inclined towards home-based economic activity and decided to pursue small farming for youth amongst the group. After founding that information technology have more activities which can be a way of reaching our target, we decide to approach different partner working in Nyamagabe District to support our initiatives.

The idea was supported by World Vision Rwanda/Nyungwe Cluster and Nyamagabe District though a Gasaka Community Knowledge Center where we have more project of ICT and getting benefit through trainings, internet services, printing and photocopying services, binding the document of all kind, organizing and supporting entertainment events, facilitate personal computer care and maintenance, Audiovisual services, farming and business information services, online platform services, library service, reading and writing service, etc.

In short our dream come true because we continue to expand our activities through the project sponsored by the Local Competitiveness Facility (LCF) which is a matched grant facility to support private business partnerships focusing on value chain integration. LCF is a fund implemented by the Local Administrative Entities Development Agency (LODA) in the Ministry of Local Government of Rwanda and the respective Districts funded by GIZ and the Government of Rwanda.

We have seen the life of cooperative change in a positive way whether the request of membership and partnership increased because of our story, we have a purpose of becoming a center of excellence in Nyamagabe District and Southern province as well. We now feel part of the community and we think we will inspire other group of people to be successful, as we share them our experience.
